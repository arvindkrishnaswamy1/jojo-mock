Arya   Rohan   Priya Mamma
Arya and the Lost Kingdom of Kishkindha

One night, the moon disappeared.

Not dimmed. Not hiding behind a cloud.

GONE.

Arya stared at the sky.

"Rohan," she whispered. "Where is the moon?"

Rohan looked up. Then back at Arya. Then up again.

"Uh oh," said Rohan.

✦ ✦ ✦

Without the moon, the whole kingdom of Kishkindha was in darkness.

The monkeys couldn't find their bananas.

The elephants kept bumping into trees.

Even the stars looked nervous.

"We have to find it," said Arya.

She grabbed her tiny torch.

Rohan grabbed his bravest face.

(It was a little wobbly. But it would do.)

✦ ✦ ✦

Then — WHOOOOSH.

A giant landed right in front of them.

It was Hanuman.

As tall as three houses. His smile wider than a river.

"You're looking for the moon," said Hanuman.

"Yes!" said Arya.

"I know where it is."

"WHERE?" shouted Arya and Rohan together.

Hanuman leaned down slowly and whispered —

"It's scared."

✦ ✦ ✦

Arya blinked.

She had never thought about that.

Even the moon could feel scared.

Just like she did sometimes. On the first day of school. Before trying something new.

"What does it need?" asked Arya softly.

"The same thing you need," said Hanuman.

"Someone kind. Someone safe. Someone to say — I see you."

✦ ✦ ✦

So Arya sat down in the dark.

She thought of what Priya Mamma always sang at bedtime.

Quietly. Very quietly.

She started to hum.

And slowly...

The sky got a little lighter.

A silver edge peeked out from behind a cloud.

Then more. And more.

The moon was back.

Round and warm and low.

Like a nightlight.

Just for them.

Rohan yawned.

Arya yawned.

Even Hanuman yawned — then pretended he didn't.

"Time to go home," Arya said quietly.

Her eyes felt heavy.

Her legs felt slow.

Everything felt still.

Arya closed her eyes.

The moon shone just for her.

Priya Mamma was nearby.

Everything was safe.

And she slept. ✦

Tonight's wisdom
"True bravery is noticing what others overlook — and acting with courage and kindness."
