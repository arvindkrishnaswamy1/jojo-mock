## What is JoJo?

JoJo is a personalized AI bedtime story app for children. Every story features the child as the star — their name, personality, favourite things, and the people they love woven into every narrative. Stories are read aloud with soothing voice narration, designed to hold children's attention and ease them gently into sleep.

> *"Sweet dream stories with Love. For every Sleep time."*

## V5: The Storytelling Engine

### Inspired by *Grumpy Monkey* and the best of children's picture books

The biggest update in V5 is how JoJo tells stories. We've moved away from adult-style paragraphs toward the short, punchy, breathing rhythm that children's picture books use — and that children respond to instinctively.

### The Four-Act Story Structure

Every JoJo story follows a deliberate arc:

**Act 1 — The Hook**
One dramatic sentence. An impossible situation. Something is missing, broken, or very wrong. Children lean in.

> *"One night, the moon disappeared. Not dimmed. Not hiding behind a cloud. GONE."*

**Act 2 — The Drama**
Short chunks of 2–4 lines. Big reactions. Humor. The absurdity of the situation escalates. Children are fully engaged.

> *"Rohan looked up. Then back at Arya. Then up again. 'Uh oh,' said Rohan."*

**Act 3 — The Moral Moment**
The lesson arrives through experience — not through a character being told what to think. The child hero figures it out herself.

> *"She had never thought about that. Even the moon could feel scared. Just like she did sometimes."*

**Act 4 — The Sleep Arc**
Energy drops. Sentences get shorter. Words get softer. Colours shift to moonlight and quiet. The story itself becomes a lullaby.

> *"Her eyes felt heavy. Her legs felt slow. Everything felt still."*

### Why this works

- **Chunked for attention spans** — no wall of text. Each moment lands before the next begins.
- **Dramatized for engagement** — children laugh, gasp, or worry before the tension resolves.
- **Morals through experience** — never preachy. The child hero discovers it herself.
- **Progressive sleep nudge** — the story's energy mirrors the child's body slowing down.

## Personalization

Every story is built around the child:

- **Child's name** as the hero, spoken throughout
- **Best friend / cousin** as the sidekick
- **Parent's role** woven naturally into the narrative (Mamma, Amma, Papa, Nani, etc.)
- **Favourite animal, place, food, and more** appear as story details
- **Personality traits** shape how the hero behaves

## Story Modes

- **Drift to Sleep** — gentle pacing, soft vocabulary, progressive dimming of energy
- **Spark Wonder** — curiosity-led, ends on a big idea
- **Moral Moment** — the lesson lands through action, never lecture
- **Routine stories** — Bath Time, Brush Teeth, Tidy Up, Be on Time, Eat Well
